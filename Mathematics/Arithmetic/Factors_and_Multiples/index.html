Factors and Multiples

Understand relationships between numbers 📊

📚 Definitions ▼

🔢 Factors

A factor of a number is a number that divides it exactly (with no remainder).

Example:

Factors of 12 are:
1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

Because:
12 ÷ 1 = 12
12 ÷ 2 = 6
12 ÷ 3 = 4
12 ÷ 4 = 3
12 ÷ 6 = 2
12 ÷ 12 = 1
💡 Quick Trick: Factors are usually smaller than or equal to the number.

✖️ Multiples

A multiple of a number is what you get when you multiply that number by whole numbers.

Example:

Multiples of 5:
5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30…

Because:
5 × 1 = 5
5 × 2 = 10
5 × 3 = 15
5 × 4 = 20
5 × 5 = 25
💡 Quick Trick: Multiples can go on forever.

🤝 Common Factors

A common factor is a factor shared by two or more numbers.

Example:

Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18

Common factors: 1, 2, 3, 6

🔄 Common Multiples

A common multiple is a multiple shared by two or more numbers.

Example:

Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24…
Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18, 24, 30…

Common multiples: 12, 24…

The smallest common multiple is called the LCM (Least Common Multiple).

🎯 Even Numbers

An even number is divisible by 2.

Examples:

2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16…
💡 Quick Trick: Even numbers end in: 0, 2, 4, 6, 8

🎲 Odd Numbers

An odd number is not divisible by 2.

Examples:

1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15…
💡 Quick Trick: Odd numbers end in: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9

✨ Prime Numbers

A prime number has exactly two factors: 1 and itself.

Examples:

2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, 23…
Important facts about primes:
2 is the only even prime number.
1 is NOT a prime number.

Example:

7 has only: 1 and 7
So it is prime.

🧩 Composite Numbers

A composite number has more than two factors.

Examples:

4, 6, 8, 9, 10, 12, 14, 15, 16…

Example:

12 has: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
So it is composite.

👯 Twin Primes

Twin primes are two prime numbers that differ by 2.

Examples:

3 and 5
5 and 7
11 and 13
17 and 19
29 and 31
Both numbers must be prime.

🎭 Prime Triplet

A prime triplet is a set of three prime numbers close together.

The only prime triplet is:

3, 5, 7

Why only one?

Among any three consecutive odd numbers, one will usually be divisible by 3.

🔐 Co-prime Numbers

Two numbers are co-prime if their only common factor is 1.

Example:

8 and 15

Factors of 8: 1, 2, 4, 8
Factors of 15: 1, 3, 5, 15

Only common factor = 1
So they are co-prime.
Important: Co-prime numbers do NOT need to be prime themselves.

Example: 8 and 9 are co-prime.

💎 Perfect Number

A perfect number is a number where the sum of its proper factors equals the number itself.

(Proper factors = all factors except the number itself.)

Example: 6

Factors of 6: 1, 2, 3, 6
Proper factors: 1, 2, 3

Sum: 1 + 2 + 3 = 6 ✓
So 6 is a perfect number.

More Examples:

28 (1 + 2 + 4 + 7 + 14 = 28)
496

⚡ Divisibility Rules ▼
📌 Divisibility by 2
A number is divisible by 2 if its last digit is even (0, 2, 4, 6, or 8).
Examples:
• 24 → Last digit is 4 (even) → Divisible by 2 ✓
• 356 → Last digit is 6 (even) → Divisible by 2 ✓
• 123 → Last digit is 3 (odd) → NOT divisible by 2 ✗
📌 Divisibility by 3
A number is divisible by 3 if the sum of all its digits is divisible by 3.
Examples:
• 123 → 1 + 2 + 3 = 6 → 6 is divisible by 3 → 123 is divisible by 3 ✓
• 456 → 4 + 5 + 6 = 15 → 15 is divisible by 3 → 456 is divisible by 3 ✓
• 124 → 1 + 2 + 4 = 7 → 7 is NOT divisible by 3 → 124 is NOT divisible by 3 ✗
📌 Divisibility by 4
A number is divisible by 4 if its last two digits form a number divisible by 4.
Examples:
• 312 → Last two digits are 12 → 12 ÷ 4 = 3 → Divisible by 4 ✓
• 2520 → Last two digits are 20 → 20 ÷ 4 = 5 → Divisible by 4 ✓
• 314 → Last two digits are 14 → 14 ÷ 4 = 3.5 → NOT divisible by 4 ✗
📌 Divisibility by 5
A number is divisible by 5 if its last digit is 0 or 5.
Examples:
• 125 → Last digit is 5 → Divisible by 5 ✓
• 340 → Last digit is 0 → Divisible by 5 ✓
• 342 → Last digit is 2 → NOT divisible by 5 ✗
📌 Divisibility by 6
A number is divisible by 6 if it is divisible by both 2 AND 3.
Examples:
• 234 → Divisible by 2 (ends in 4) AND sum 2+3+4=9 (divisible by 3) → Divisible by 6 ✓
• 120 → Divisible by 2 (ends in 0) AND sum 1+2+0=3 (divisible by 3) → Divisible by 6 ✓
• 123 → Divisible by 3 but NOT by 2 (ends in 3) → NOT divisible by 6 ✗
📌 Divisibility by 7
Method: Remove the last digit, double it, and subtract from the remaining number. If the result is divisible by 7, then the original number is divisible by 7.
Examples:
• 343 → Remove 3, double it (6), subtract from 34: 34 - 6 = 28 → 28 ÷ 7 = 4 → Divisible by 7 ✓
• 119 → Remove 9, double it (18), subtract from 11: 11 - 18 = -7 → -7 ÷ 7 = -1 → Divisible by 7 ✓
• 345 → Remove 5, double it (10), subtract from 34: 34 - 10 = 24 → 24 ÷ 7 = 3.4... → NOT divisible by 7 ✗
📌 Divisibility by 8
A number is divisible by 8 if its last three digits form a number divisible by 8.
Examples:
• 2536 → Last three digits are 536 → 536 ÷ 8 = 67 → Divisible by 8 ✓
• 5432 → Last three digits are 432 → 432 ÷ 8 = 54 → Divisible by 8 ✓
• 5433 → Last three digits are 433 → 433 ÷ 8 = 54.125 → NOT divisible by 8 ✗
📌 Divisibility by 9
A number is divisible by 9 if the sum of all its digits is divisible by 9.
Examples:
• 234 → 2 + 3 + 4 = 9 → 9 is divisible by 9 → 234 is divisible by 9 ✓
• 729 → 7 + 2 + 9 = 18 → 18 is divisible by 9 → 729 is divisible by 9 ✓
• 234 → 2 + 3 + 4 = 9 (wait, this was ✓). Let's try 235 → 2 + 3 + 5 = 10 → NOT divisible by 9 ✗
📌 Divisibility by 10
A number is divisible by 10 if its last digit is 0.
Examples:
• 240 → Last digit is 0 → Divisible by 10 ✓
• 3450 → Last digit is 0 → Divisible by 10 ✓
• 345 → Last digit is 5 → NOT divisible by 10 ✗
📌 Divisibility by 11
A number is divisible by 11 if the difference between the sum of digits in odd positions and sum of digits in even positions is divisible by 11 (or equals 0).
Examples:
• 121 → Odd positions (1st, 3rd): 1 + 1 = 2 → Even positions (2nd): 2 → Difference: 2 - 2 = 0 → Divisible by 11 ✓
• 1221 → Odd positions: 1 + 2 = 3 → Even positions: 2 + 1 = 3 → Difference: 3 - 3 = 0 → Divisible by 11 ✓
• 123 → Odd positions: 1 + 3 = 4 → Even positions: 2 → Difference: 4 - 2 = 2 → NOT divisible by 11 ✗

⚡ General Properties of Divisibility

✨ Property 1
If a number is divisible by another number, then it is divisible by all factors of that number.
Example:
36 is divisible by 12.
So 36 is also divisible by all factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, and 12 ✓
✨ Property 2
If a number is divisible by two co-prime numbers, then it is divisible by their product.
(Co-prime numbers have only 1 as a common factor.)
Example 1:
30 is divisible by 3 and 5.
Since 3 and 5 are co-prime, 30 is also divisible by: 3 × 5 = 15 ✓

Example 2:
84 is divisible by 4 and 7.
Since 4 and 7 are co-prime, 84 is divisible by: 4 × 7 = 28 ✓

Important Note:
This works only when the numbers are co-prime.
Example: 12 is divisible by 3 and 6, but NOT by 18 (because 3 and 6 are not co-prime) ✗
✨ Property 3
If two numbers are divisible by a number, then their sum is also divisible by that number.
Example:
20 and 44 are divisible by 4.
20 + 44 = 64
64 is also divisible by 4 ✓ (64 ÷ 4 = 16)
✨ Property 4
If two numbers are divisible by a number, then their difference is also divisible by that number.
Example:
44 and 20 are divisible by 4.
44 − 20 = 24
24 is also divisible by 4 ✓ (24 ÷ 4 = 6)
🔍 Deciding Whether a Number is Prime ▼

What is a Prime Number?

A prime number has exactly two factors:

1
itself

How to Check if a Number is Prime:

Step 1: Find the square root range

Find the largest natural number m such that: m × m ≤ n

(This means: Find the approximate square root of your number.)

Step 2: List all prime numbers

List all prime numbers less than or equal to m.

Step 3: Check divisibility

Check whether the number is divisible by any of those prime numbers.

If divisible → NOT prime ✗
If not divisible → PRIME ✓
📝 Example 1: Is 137 Prime?
Step 1: Find m
11 × 11 = 121 ✓
12 × 12 = 144 (too large)
So m = 11

Step 2: Prime numbers up to 11:
2, 3, 5, 7, 11

Step 3: Check divisibility
137 is not divisible by any of them.

Answer: 137 is PRIME ✓
📝 Example 2: Is 331 Prime?
Step 1: Find m
18 × 18 = 324 ✓
19 × 19 = 361 (too large)
So m = 18

Step 2: Prime numbers up to 18:
2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17

Step 3: Check divisibility
331 is not divisible by any of them.

Answer: 331 is PRIME ✓
📝 Example 3: Is 527 Prime?
Step 1: Find m
22 × 22 = 484 ✓
23 × 23 = 529 (too large)
So m = 22

Step 2: Prime numbers up to 22:
2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19

Step 3: Check divisibility
527 is divisible by 17!
17 × 31 = 527

Answer: 527 is NOT PRIME ✗
