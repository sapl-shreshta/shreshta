🌱 Level 1: Intuitive Understanding (Early Concepts)

Start with what an angle is, without formal definitions.

What is an angle? (two lines meeting at a point)
Angles in real life (clock, door, scissors, pizza slice)
Turning and rotation (quarter turn, half turn, full turn)
Bigger vs smaller angles (visual comparison only)

🧱 Level 2: Basic Definitions

Introduce proper terms and structure.

Vertex, arms (sides)
Naming angles (∠ABC format)
Types of angles:
Acute (< 90°)
Right (= 90°)
Obtuse (90°–180°)
Straight (= 180°)
Reflex (> 180°)

📏 Level 3: Measuring Angles

Make it hands-on and visual.

Units: degrees (°)
Full circle = 360°
Using a protractor
Estimating angles
Drawing angles of given measure

🔄 Level 4: Angle Relationships (Foundation Logic)

This is where reasoning starts.

Adjacent angles
Linear pair (sum = 180°)
Vertically opposite angles (equal)
Complementary angles (sum = 90°)
Supplementary angles (sum = 180°)

📐 Level 5: Angles in Shapes

Connect angles to geometry.

Angles in triangles (sum = 180°)
Types of triangles based on angles
Angles in quadrilaterals (sum = 360°)
Regular polygons and their angles

✂️ Level 6: Angles with Lines & Transversals

This is a big conceptual jump.

Parallel lines
Transversal line
Corresponding angles (equal)
Alternate interior angles (equal)
Co-interior angles (sum = 180°)

🔁 Level 7: Angles in Circles

More visual + slightly abstract.

Central angle
Inscribed angle
Angle in a semicircle (90°)
Angles in the same segment
Tangent and radius angle (90°)

📊 Level 8: Introduction to Trigonometry

Now angles connect to ratios.

What is trigonometry (angles + sides)
Sine, cosine, tangent (basic idea)
Right triangle relationships
Using angles to find height/distance

🔬 Level 9: Advanced Angle Concepts

For deeper math thinking.

Angle of elevation & depression
Bearings (navigation)
Radians (alternative to degrees)
Unit circle (angles + coordinates)

🧠 Level 10: Abstract & Problem Solving

Where mastery happens.

Multi-step angle problems
Algebra with angles (solve for x)
Proof-based problems (why rules work)
Real-world applications (architecture, design)
