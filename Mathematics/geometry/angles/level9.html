🔢 What is Trigonometry?

Trigonometry is the branch of mathematics that studies the relationships between angles and sides in triangles.

The word comes from Greek: "tri" (three) + "gon" (angle) + "metry" (measurement).

Why learn it? Trigonometry helps you:

  • Find unknown side lengths in triangles
  • Find unknown angles when you know side lengths
  • Solve real-world problems (heights, distances, navigation)
  • Understand waves, circles, and periodic motion

📐 Right Triangles: The Foundation

Trigonometry is built around right triangles (triangles with one 90° angle).

Right Triangle with angle θ

The Three Main Parts (relative to angle θ)

  • Hypotenuse: The longest side, opposite the right angle
  • Opposite: The side across from your angle θ
  • Adjacent: The side next to your angle θ (not the hypotenuse)

📊 The Three Trigonometric Ratios

The three main trigonometric functions compare sides of a right triangle:

Sine (sin)

sin(θ) = Opposite / Hypotenuse

Ratio of opposite side to hypotenuse

Cosine (cos)

cos(θ) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse

Ratio of adjacent side to hypotenuse

Tangent (tan)

tan(θ) = Opposite / Adjacent

Ratio of opposite to adjacent

💡 Memory Aid: SOH CAH TOA

Sine = Opposite/Hypotenuse

Cosine = Adjacent/Hypotenuse

Tangent = Opposite/Adjacent

📈 Example Problem

Given: Right triangle with angle θ = 30°, hypotenuse = 10 units

Find: The opposite side length

Using sine ratio:
sin(30°) = opposite / 10
0.5 = opposite / 10
opposite = 5 units

  • ✈️ Navigation: Flight paths, bearings, and GPS systems
  • 🌊 Physics: Waves, sound, and light behavior
  • 🎮 Computer Graphics: 3D rotations and transformations
