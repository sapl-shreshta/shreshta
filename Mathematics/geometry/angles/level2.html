Level 2: Parts of an Angle

Learn the anatomy of angles! 🎯

🏗️ The Three Main Parts of an Angle

Every angle has exactly three important parts:

The Three Parts

1. 📍 Vertex

  • Definition: The point where the two lines meet
  • Remember: It's the corner or intersection point
  • Example: The point at the center of a clock

2. 📏 Arms (or Sides)

  • Definition: The two lines (or rays) that form the angle
  • Remember: They start from the vertex and extend outward
  • Both arms are important: You need both to have an angle
  • Example: The two hands of a clock are the arms

3. 🌊 The Opening

  • Definition: The space between the two arms
  • Remember: The size of this opening determines the angle size
  • Measured in: Degrees (°)

📝 How to Name Angles

Angles are named using three letters or one number:

Angle Notation: ∠ABC

  • ∠ = This symbol means "angle"
  • A and C = Points on the two arms
  • B = The vertex (always in the middle!)
  • Example: ∠ABC = "angle ABC" (vertex at B)

💡 Important: The vertex letter must be in the middle!

  • ✓ Correct: ∠ABC (B is the vertex)
  • ✗ Wrong: ∠CAB (looks like A is the vertex)

📊 Five Types of Angles (Classification)

Angles are classified by their size. Let's explore all five types:

Acute Angle

Less than 90°

Sharp and pointy, like a pencil tip!

Right Angle

Exactly 90°

Perfect corner, like a square!

Obtuse Angle

Between 90° and 180°

Wide open, like an open door!

Straight Angle

Exactly 180°

Forms a straight line!

Reflex Angle

Between 180° and 360°

Very wide open, the "big angle"!

🎓 Quick Summary Table

Here's a handy summary of all five angle types:

  • 🔸 Acute Angle: 0° to 90° (sharp)
  • 📐 Right Angle: exactly 90° (corner)
  • 🔶 Obtuse Angle: 90° to 180° (wide)
  • ➡️ Straight Angle: exactly 180° (line)
  • 🔵 Reflex Angle: 180° to 360° (very wide)

💡 Real-Life Examples of Angle Types

  • Acute: The angle of a clock hand at 1:00, a slice of pizza, open scissors at a small angle
  • Right: The corners of a book, window, or room, the "+" symbol
  • Obtuse: A clock hand at 10:00, an open laptop screen, a wide-open door
  • Straight: A closed book (flat), a horizon line, a ruler
  • Reflex: The large angle when you open a door more than 180°, a boomerang shape
