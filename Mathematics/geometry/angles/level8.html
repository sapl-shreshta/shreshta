Problem 1 Easy

Triangle Problem: In a triangle, two angles are 45° and 60°. What is the third angle?

Solution: Sum of angles in a triangle = 180°
Third angle = 180° - 45° - 60° = 75°
Problem 2 Easy

Linear Pair: Two adjacent angles form a linear pair. One angle is 120°. What is the other?

Solution: Linear pair angles sum to 180°
Second angle = 180° - 120° = 60°
Problem 3 Easy

Complementary Angles: Two angles are complementary. One is 35°. What is the complement?

Solution: Complementary angles sum to 90°
Complement = 90° - 35° = 55°
Problem 4 Medium

Quadrilateral: A quadrilateral has angles 80°, 100°, and 90°. Find the fourth angle.

Solution: Sum of angles in a quadrilateral = 360°
Fourth angle = 360° - 80° - 100° - 90° = 90°
Problem 5 Medium

Vertical Angles: Two lines intersect forming four angles. One angle is 65°. What are the other three angles?

Solution: Vertical angles are equal, and adjacent angles form linear pairs
Opposite angle = 65° (vertical angles)
Adjacent angles = 180° - 65° = 115° each
So the four angles are: 65°, 115°, 65°, 115°
Problem 6 Medium

Pentagon: In a regular pentagon, what is each interior angle? (Formula: (n-2)×180°÷n, where n=5)

Solution: Using the formula: (5-2)×180°÷5
= 3 × 180° ÷ 5
= 540° ÷ 5
= 108°
Problem 7 Hard

Parallel Lines & Transversal: A transversal crosses two parallel lines. If one angle is 70°, what are the corresponding angle, alternate interior angle, and co-interior angle?

Solution:
• Corresponding angle = 70° (equal to original)
• Alternate interior angle = 70° (equal)
• Co-interior angle = 180° - 70° = 110° (supplementary)
Problem 8 Hard

Complex Problem: In triangle ABC, angle A = 50°. Angles B and C are supplementary to external angles that are both 70°. What are angles B and C?

Solution:
If external angle = 70°, then internal angle = 180° - 70° = 110°
So angles B = C = 110° (both are this angle)
Check: 50° + 110° + 110° = 270° ≠ 180°
