Level 5: Angles in Shapes

Explore angles inside polygons! 📊

🔺 Triangles: The Three-Angle Shapes

Every triangle has exactly three angles, and they have an amazing property:

⭐ The Triangle Angle Sum Rule: All three angles in any triangle add up to exactly 180°

Angle A + Angle B + Angle C = 180°

50° + 60° + 70° = 180°

Triangle Types by Angles

  • Acute Triangle: All three angles are less than 90° (e.g., 50°, 60°, 70°)
  • Right Triangle: One angle is exactly 90°, the other two are acute (e.g., 90°, 45°, 45°)
  • Obtuse Triangle: One angle is greater than 90°, the other two are acute (e.g., 120°, 40°, 20°)

💡 Using the Triangle Rule

  • Find a missing angle: If you know two angles, subtract their sum from 180°
  • Example: In a triangle with angles 60° and 70°, the third angle = 180° - 60° - 70° = 50°
  • Check your work: Make sure all three angles of any triangle sum to exactly 180°

⬜ Quadrilaterals: The Four-Angle Shapes

Quadrilaterals have four angles, and they follow their own special rule:

⭐ The Quadrilateral Angle Sum Rule: All four angles in any quadrilateral add up to 360°

Angle A + Angle B + Angle C + Angle D = 360°

Common Quadrilaterals

  • Square: All four angles are 90° (90° + 90° + 90° + 90° = 360°)
  • Rectangle: All four angles are 90°, same as square
  • Parallelogram: Opposite angles are equal
  • Trapezoid: Only one pair of parallel sides
  • Irregular Quadrilateral: Four angles of different sizes that sum to 360°
Square: Sum = 360°

🔷 Regular Polygons

A regular polygon is a shape where all sides are equal and all angles are equal.

Formula for interior angle in regular polygon:

Each angle = (n - 2) × 180° ÷ n

Where n = number of sides

Examples

  • Equilateral Triangle (n=3): Each angle = (3-2)×180°÷3 = 60°
  • Square (n=4): Each angle = (4-2)×180°÷4 = 90°
  • Pentagon (n=5): Each angle = (5-2)×180°÷5 = 108°
  • Hexagon (n=6): Each angle = (6-2)×180°÷6 = 120°
  • Octagon (n=8): Each angle = (8-2)×180°÷8 = 135°
