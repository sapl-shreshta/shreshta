📚 Fundamentals of Angles ▼

📐 What is an Angle?

An angle is formed when two lines meet at a point. That point is called the vertex.

Angles are everywhere around you:

  • 🏠 The corners of your room
  • 📐 The corner of your notebook
  • ⏰ The hands of a clock
  • 🍕 A slice of pizza
  • ✂️ Open scissors

📏 Types of Angles

Angles are measured in degrees (°). Different angles have different names:

  • Right Angle: Exactly 90° (like a corner of a square) ⊞
  • Acute Angle: Less than 90° (sharp and pointy)
  • Obtuse Angle: Between 90° and 180° (wide and open)
  • Straight Angle: Exactly 180° (forms a straight line)
  • Reflex Angle: Between 180° and 360° (extra wide!)

🎯 How to Measure Angles?

We use a tool called a protractor to measure angles!

Steps to measure an angle:

  • Place the center of the protractor at the vertex (the point where lines meet)
  • Align one line of the angle with the zero line of the protractor
  • Read the number where the other line crosses the protractor
  • That's your angle in degrees! ✨

💡 Fun Facts about Angles!

  • A full rotation around a point is 360 degrees! 🔄
  • The angles in a triangle always add up to 180 degrees! 📐
  • A circle has angles that go from 0° to 360°! 🔵
  • Protractors have been used for hundreds of years to measure angles! 📜

🔗 Angle Relationships ▼

👫 Adjacent Angles

Adjacent angles are two angles that share a relationship:

  • Share a common vertex (corner point)
  • Share a common side
  • Do not overlap

💡 Key Idea: Adjacent angles are next to each other.

Adjacent Angles Example

Example: If one angle is 30° and the next angle is 50°, they are adjacent angles.

📏 Linear Pair

A linear pair is a special type of adjacent angles:

  • They are adjacent angles
  • Their non-common sides form a straight line

💡 Key Rule: Angles in a linear pair always add up to 180°.

Linear Pair: 110° + 70° = 180°

Example: If one angle is 110°, the other angle is:

180° − 110° = 70°

🔄 Vertically Opposite Angles

When two lines intersect, they form pairs of opposite angles:

  • These angles are across from each other
  • They are not adjacent

💡 Key Rule: Vertically opposite angles are always equal.

Vertically Opposite: Equal angles

Example: If one angle is 75°, the opposite angle is also 75°.

✨ Complementary Angles

Two angles are complementary if their sum equals 90°.

💡 Key Rule: Complementary angles add up to 90°.

Complementary: 40° + 50° = 90°

Example: 40° + 50° = 90° ✓

➕ Supplementary Angles

Two angles are supplementary if their sum equals 180°.

💡 Key Rule: Supplementary angles add up to 180°.

Supplementary: 120° + 60° = 180°

Example: 120° + 60° = 180° ✓

📝 Quick Summary

  • 👫 Adjacent Angles → Next to each other
  • 📏 Linear Pair → Adjacent + straight line = 180°
  • 🔄 Vertically Opposite Angles → Equal angles
  • ✨ Complementary Angles → Sum = 90°
  • ➕ Supplementary Angles → Sum = 180°
