Level 4: Angle Relationships

Discover how angles relate to each other! 🎯

👫 Adjacent Angles

Adjacent angles are two angles that share a common vertex and share a common side, but do not overlap.

Adjacent Angles (40° + 50°)
  • Key feature: They share one common side (the middle line)
  • Their sum: Can be any value - they don't have a fixed total
  • Real example: Slices in a pizza are adjacent angles

📏 Linear Pair

A linear pair is a special case of adjacent angles where the two non-common sides form a straight line.

Linear Pair: 110° + 70° = 180°
⭐ Key Rule: Linear Pair Angles Always Sum to 180°

If two angles form a linear pair: Angle 1 + Angle 2 = 180°

Example: 110° + 70° = 180°

  • What it is: Two adjacent angles on a straight line
  • The rule: They always add to exactly 180°
  • Use it to find: If you know one angle, subtract from 180° to find the other

🔄 Vertically Opposite Angles

When two straight lines cross, they form four angles. The angles that are directly across from each other are called vertically opposite angles.

Vertical Angles: A=C, B=D
⭐ Key Rule: Vertically Opposite Angles Are Always Equal

If two lines intersect: Angle A = Angle C, and Angle B = Angle D

  • What it is: Opposite angles formed when two lines cross
  • The rule: They are always equal in measure
  • Bonus: Adjacent angles at the intersection form linear pairs

✨ Complementary Angles

Two angles are complementary if their sum equals 90°. They don't need to be adjacent!

Complementary: 30° + 60° = 90°
Complementary: 45° + 45° = 90°
⭐ Key Rule: Complementary Angles Sum to 90°

Angle A + Angle B = 90°

Examples: 30° + 60° = 90°, or 45° + 45° = 90°

  • Key requirement: The two angles must sum to exactly 90°
  • They can be: Adjacent or separate
  • Use it to find: Subtract from 90° to find its complement

➕ Supplementary Angles

Two angles are supplementary if their sum equals 180°.

Supplementary: 120° + 60° = 180°
Supplementary: 90° + 90° = 180°
⭐ Key Rule: Supplementary Angles Sum to 180°

Angle A + Angle B = 180°

Examples: 120° + 60° = 180°, or 90° + 90° = 180°

  • Key requirement: The two angles must sum to exactly 180°
  • Connection: Linear pairs are always supplementary!
  • Use it to find: Subtract from 180° to find its supplement

🎓 What You've Learned in Level 4

  • ✓ Adjacent angles share a vertex and a side
  • ✓ Linear pairs sum to 180° and form a straight line
  • ✓ Vertically opposite angles are equal
  • ✓ Complementary angles sum to 90°
  • ✓ Supplementary angles sum to 180°
