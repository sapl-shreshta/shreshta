Level 6: Parallel Lines & Transversals

Discover powerful angle relationships! 🎯

∥ Parallel Lines

Parallel lines are two or more lines that never meet, no matter how far they extend. They stay the same distance apart forever!

Symbol: We write ∥ to show parallel lines (e.g., line AB ∥ line CD)

Real examples: Railroad tracks, lane markers on roads, bars on a ladder, edges of a notebook

✂️ Transversal

A transversal is a line that crosses (intersects) two or more other lines at different points.

When a transversal crosses two parallel lines, it creates eight angles (four at each intersection point). These angles have special relationships!

Transversal crossing parallel lines

🔗 Corresponding Angles

Corresponding angles are angles in the same position at each intersection.

⭐ Rule: When a transversal crosses parallel lines, corresponding angles are EQUAL

Example: Angle 1 = Angle 5, Angle 2 = Angle 6, etc.

  • They're on the same side of the transversal
  • One is at the top intersection, one is at the bottom intersection
  • They have the same position relative to their intersection

🔀 Alternate Interior Angles

Alternate interior angles are angles that are:

  • On opposite sides of the transversal
  • Between (interior to) the two parallel lines
⭐ Rule: Alternate interior angles are EQUAL

Example: If one alternate interior angle is 70°, the other is also 70°

↔️ Co-Interior Angles

Co-interior angles (also called consecutive interior or same-side interior) are angles that are:

  • On the same side of the transversal
  • Between (interior to) the two parallel lines
⭐ Rule: Co-interior angles are SUPPLEMENTARY (sum to 180°)

Example: If one co-interior angle is 120°, the other is 180° - 120° = 60°

📊 Angle Relationship Summary

  • Corresponding angles: Same position at each intersection = EQUAL
  • Alternate interior angles: Opposite sides, between lines = EQUAL
  • Alternate exterior angles: Opposite sides, outside lines = EQUAL
  • Co-interior angles: Same side, between lines = SUM TO 180°
