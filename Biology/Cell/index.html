Cell

The Basic Unit of Life 🌿

📝 MCQ Worksheet (100 Questions)

🔍 What is a Cell?

A cell is the basic structural and functional unit of a living organism. According to cell theory postulates, a cell is the basic building block of life, which makes anything alive and is self-sufficient to carry out all the fundamental functions of an organism.

Key Points:
Cells are the smallest units of life
All living organisms are made of cells
Cells reproduce to form new cells
Cells contain genetic material (DNA)

🏗️ What are Cell Organelles?

The cellular components are called cell organelles. These cell organelles include both membrane and non-membrane bound organelles, present within the cells and are distinct in their structures and functions. They coordinate and function efficiently for the normal functioning of the cell.

A few of them function by providing shape and support, whereas some are involved in the locomotion and reproduction of a cell. There are various organelles present within the cell and are classified into three categories based on the presence or absence of membrane.

🔴 Organelles Without Membrane
Non-membrane-bound organelles:
Cell Wall
Ribosomes
Cytoskeleton

Found in both prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells

🟡 Single Membrane-Bound Organelles
Single membrane-bound organelles:
Vacuole
Lysosome
Golgi Apparatus
Endoplasmic Reticulum

Found only in eukaryotic cells

🔵 Double Membrane-Bound Organelles
Double membrane-bound organelles:
Nucleus
Mitochondria
Chloroplast

Found only in eukaryotic cells

📋 List of Cell Organelles and Their Functions

Plasma Membrane (Cell Membrane / Cytoplasmic Membrane)

The plasma membrane is a selectively permeable membrane of the cells, which is composed of a lipid bilayer and proteins.

Functions:

Acts as a selectively permeable membrane
Permits entry of selective materials in and out of the cell
Provides shape to the cell
Protects the inner contents of the cell

Based on the fluid mosaic model: made of a lipid bilayer with embedded protein molecules

Cytoplasm

The cytoplasm is a jelly-like substance found between the cell membrane and nucleus. It is mainly composed of water, organic and inorganic compounds.

Functions:

Serves as the medium where all cell organelles are embedded
Contains enzymes responsible for controlling metabolic activities
Site for most chemical reactions within the cell

Nucleus

The nucleus is a double-membraned organelle found in all eukaryotic cells. It is the largest organelle, which functions as the control centre of the cellular activities.

Key Structures:

Nuclear membrane (porous, like cell membrane)
Nucleolus (tiny spherical bodies)
Chromosomes (carry genes)
DNA (genetic material)

Functions:

Controls cellular activities, metabolism, and growth
Stores the cell's DNA
Nucleoli synthesize protein and RNA
Controls hereditary characters and cell functions

🔋 Mitochondria (Powerhouse of the Cell)

Mitochondria are called the powerhouses of the cell as they produce energy-rich molecules. They are double membrane-bound, sausage-shaped organelles, found in almost all eukaryotic cells.

Structure:

Outer membrane (continuous boundary with cytoplasm)
Inner membrane (folded into cristae)
Matrix (inner compartment)

Functions:

Sites of aerobic respiration
Produces energy in the form of ATP
Helps transform molecules (e.g., glucose → ATP)

Has their own circular DNA, RNA, ribosomes (70s), and molecules for protein synthesis

🌿 Chloroplasts

Chloroplasts are double membrane-bound organelles which store chlorophyll and other carotenoid pigments. They are responsible for photosynthesis.

Key Structures:

Stroma (inner space)
Thylakoids (flattened disc-like structures containing chlorophyll)
Grana (stacked thylakoids)
Stromal lamella (connect thylakoids of different grana)

Functions:

Sites of photosynthesis
Traps light energy for photosynthesis
Synthesis of carbohydrates and proteins

Contains double-stranded circular DNA, 70S ribosomes, and enzymes

Endoplasmic Reticulum

The Endoplasmic Reticulum is a network of membranous canals filled with fluid. They are the transport system of the cell.

Types:

Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum
Composed of cisternae, tubules, and vesicles
Involved in protein manufacture
Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum
Storage organelle
Produces lipids and steroids
Responsible for detoxifying the cell

Golgi Apparatus (Golgi Complex)

Golgi Apparatus is a membrane-bound organelle, composed of a series of flattened, stacked pouches called cisternae.

Functions:

Transporting proteins and lipids
Modifying proteins and lipids
Packaging to targeted destinations

Found in both plant and animal cells

Lysosomes (Suicidal Bags 🧬)

Lysosomes are tiny, circular-shaped, single membrane-bound organelles, filled with digestive enzymes.

Functions:

Helps in digestion of cellular materials
Removes wastes
Digests dead and damaged cells

Called "suicide bags" because they can break down or digest all wastes and damaged cells

Ribosomes

Ribosomes are non-membrane-bound organelles found in close association with the endoplasmic reticulum. They are composed of 2/3rd RNA and 1/3rd protein.

Types:

70S (found in prokaryotes)
80S (found in eukaryotes)

Functions:

Protein synthesis in all living cells
Ensures survival of the cell

Found either within the endoplasmic reticulum or freely floating in the cytoplasm

Vacuoles

Vacuoles are storage bubbles of irregular shapes which are fluid-filled organelles enclosed by a membrane.

Functions:

Stores food or nutrients needed for cell survival
Stores waste products
Protects the cell from contamination

Plant cells have larger vacuoles compared to animal cells

Cytoskeleton

It is a continuous network of filamentous proteinaceous structures that run throughout the cytoplasm, from the nucleus to the plasma membrane.

Functions:

Provides shape to the cell
Provides mechanical resistance against deformation
Helps in cell motility during cytokinesis

Found in all living cells, notably in eukaryotes

Cilia and Flagella

Cilia are hair-like projections, small structures, present outside the cell wall. Flagella are slightly bigger structures.

Functions:

Work like oars to move the cell
Move the extracellular fluid
Responsible for cell movements

The core is called an axoneme, which contains nine pairs of peripheral microtubules and central microtubules

Centrosome and Centrioles

The centrosome organelle is made up of two mutually perpendicular structures known as centrioles.

Structure:

Each centriole composed of 9 equally spaced peripheral fibrils
Hub (proteinaceous core)
Radial spokes (connect hub to peripheral fibrils)

Functions:

Organize microtubules
Give rise to basal bodies of cilia and flagella
Give rise to spindle fibres during cell division

Found only in animal cells

Plastids

Plastids are large, double membrane-bound organelles which contain pigments. Based on the type of pigments, plastids are of three types:

Types:

Chloroplasts
Green coloured pigments (chlorophyll)
Sites of photosynthesis
Chromoplasts
Yellow, orange, red coloured pigments (carotenoids)
Give colour to flowers and fruits
Leucoplasts
Colorless plastids
Store starch, proteins, and oils

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Which cell organelle is called the Powerhouse of the cell?
A: Mitochondria is the cell organelle and is called the Powerhouse of the cell as they carry out the cellular respiration and generate the energy molecules called ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate).
Q: Where do we find Chloroplasts and Chromoplast pigments in plants?
A: Chloroplasts contain the green colour pigments, present in the leaves, green-coloured stems, etc. Chromoplasts contain the colourful pigments present in all colourful parts of the plant like flowers and fruits.
Q: Why are Lysosomes known as suicide bags?
A: Lysosomes are called the suicidal bags because they are capable of breaking down or digesting all the wastes, dead and damaged cells.
Q: What is Nucleoid?
A: Nucleoid is a non-membrane, irregular-shaped cell organelle present in all prokaryotic cells. They are the carriers of the genetic material of a cell.
Q: What is the largest membrane-bound organelle in a eukaryotic cell?
A: The Nucleus is the largest membrane-bound organelle in a eukaryotic cell.
