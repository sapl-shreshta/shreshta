Manasa Mandira Vasini

Devi song honoring the Mother Goddess

Raga: Sallapa

Tala: Adi

Type: Devi Keerthana (Devotional prayer to the Divine Mother)

📍 PALLAVI (Main Theme)
Manasa Mandira Vasini
Bhasaya Visala Komala Kanti
Madalase Siva Sundari
Vardhaya Nija Atma Bhavana Kirana
Word-by-Word Translation: Manasa Mandira Vasini: Oh! the one who resides in the temple of my mind
Bhasaya: Please kindle
Visala: widespread
Komala: gentle/soft/smooth (like the full moon's light)
Kanti: light (wisdom, knowledge, bliss)
Madalase: Oh Madalasa! (the one with passionate, dozy eyes)
Siva Sundari: the consort of Lord Siva
Vardhaya: Please increase/instill
Nija Atma Bhavana Kirana: the light ray of true feeling of the self (the soul)

Meaning: Oh Mother who dwells in the temple of my heart, please kindle the gentle, widespread light of wisdom and bliss. O Madalasa, beautiful consort of Siva, please increase within me the rays of true self-realization.
📍 CARANA 1 (First Verse)
Parashiva Dayite!
Vikasa Bharite!
Nana Jiva Kare!
Avagati Mudite!
Ananya Vidite!
Acale! Amale! Devi!
Word-by-Word Translation: Parashiva Dayite: Oh! beloved of Siva, the Supreme!
Vikasa Bharite: Oh! the one overflowing with bliss and light
Nana Jiva Kare: In all forms of living beings
Avagati Mudite: I understand/realize (that)
Ananya Vidite: You cannot be known by any other means; you are beyond human understanding
Acale: You are immobile, eternal (Eka Tattva - unchanging Reality)
Amale: Oh! the one without any impurities (absolute and pristine purity)
Devi: Oh Divine Mother!

Meaning: Oh beloved of Shiva! You exist in all living beings as pure consciousness and bliss. You are beyond all comprehension and words, eternal and immaculate. I bow to you, O Divine Mother!
📍 CARANA 2 (Second Verse)
Vilasasi Bahudhā
Suyoga Saranau
Buddher Vāchā Pāre
Paramapi Madhure
Subhakti Rucire
Saranau Varaniyāsi
Word-by-Word Translation: Vilasasi Bahudhā: You are glorified in many forms and many ways
Suyoga Saranau: You are the great yogic energy flow yourself
Buddher Vāchā Pāre: You are beyond intellect and words; incomprehensible through mind and speech
Paramapi Madhure: Though you are so sweet in nature
Subhakti Rucire: adorable through devotion and love
Saranau Varaniyāsi: You are the best of all (of the highest qualities)

Meaning: You manifest in countless forms and ways, as the supreme yogic energy. Though beyond the reach of mind and words, your sweetness is felt by the devoted heart. You are the highest goal, accessible only through sincere devotion.
📍 CARANA 3 (Third Verse)
Sulalita Hridaye!
Sadaiva Sadaye!
Nūnam Tvam Me Mātā
Guravararupē!
Sarasasanlāpe!
Bhavati Sacidānandā
Word-by-Word Translation: Sulalita Hridaye: O you with a very soft, compassionate heart
Sadaiva Sadaye: Always full of kindness and grace (Karuna)
Nūnam Tvam Me Mātā: Truly, you are my mother
Guravararupē: You are the highest form of a Guru (the master who dispels ignorance)
Sarasasanlāpe: charming and expressive of poetical sentiment; full of love
Bhavati Sacidānandā: You are Sat-Chit-Ananda (Truth + Consciousness + Bliss)

Meaning: O Mother with a tender, compassionate heart! You are eternally kind and merciful. You are my true mother and perfect Guru. Through your graceful expression of love, you reveal yourself as Sat-Chit-Ananda - the eternal, conscious, blissful reality.
