Molecules Matter!

Exploring the Tiny Particles of Matter 🚀

🔬 What is Chemistry?

Chemistry is the study of:

  • Matter — anything that has mass and takes up space
  • What matter is made of — atoms and molecules
  • What matter does — how it changes and reacts

💧 Water is a perfect example of matter!

⚛️ Atoms and Molecules

🔵 ATOMS

The smallest building block of matter

Cannot be broken down into smaller parts

🩷 MOLECULES

Two or more atoms bonded together

Makes up everything around us

💧 Experiment 1: Water Drops

Hanging Drop: Water beads up and holds together even while hanging

What We Observe

  • Water holds together tightly
  • Hard to split into separate drops
  • Drops combine easily when they touch
  • Shape forms a bead on wax paper

🤝 Why Do Water Molecules Stick?

Water Molecules are Attracted to Each Other

  • Molecules are attracted (pulled toward each other)
  • Molecules move and collide constantly
  • They stay close but don't stay in one place

This is why water:

  • ✓ Holds together tightly
  • ✓ Beads up in drops
  • ✓ Is hard to separate
  • ✓ Flows but stays together

🎨 Experiment 2: Molecules in Motion

Observation:

Food coloring slowly spreads through water without stirring

Why This Happens

  1. Water molecules are always moving
  2. They bump into food coloring molecules
  3. Push them in all directions
  4. Eventually spreads everywhere

🌟 Proof: Molecules are in constant motion! 🌟

🌍 Three States of Matter

❄️ SOLID

Particles packed tightly in fixed positions

💧 LIQUID

Particles close but can move around

💨 GAS

Particles spread far apart, move freely

🎓 Key Concepts

  • 🧬 Matter is Made of Particles: All matter is made of atoms and molecules
  • ⚡ Particles are in Motion: They move constantly and have energy
  • 🤝 Particles Attract: This is why water holds together
  • 🔍 Explains Everything: Molecular behavior explains the properties we observe in everyday life
